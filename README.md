# Judicial Communications Integrity Act

This repository contains the source text for the **Judicial Communications Integrity Act**, an open-source model legislative proposal.

## What is this?

The Judicial Communications Integrity Act is a model federal statute governing official executive-agency communications about federal judges, federal courts, and judicial rulings.

It is designed as an internal official-communications standard for federal executive agencies.

## Why should I care?

Federal agencies may disagree with judicial rulings. They may appeal, seek stays, explain legal disagreement, and communicate operational consequences.

But official agency communications also carry public authority. When an agency uses official channels to personally attack judges, materially misstate rulings, or imply that lawful court orders may be ignored outside lawful process, the problem is not ordinary criticism. It is institutional damage.

## What is the claim?

The core claim is that official agency communications about federal courts should remain accurate, record-tethered, nonpersonal, and institutionally responsible while preserving lawful disagreement and public legal explanation.

The Act requires agencies to distinguish legal disagreement from compliance obligation, preserve lawful review channels, correct improper communications without erasing the public record, and maintain standards that guide future official communications.

## What is this not?

This is not a general speech code.

It does not regulate private-capacity speech, campaign speech, journalism, academic commentary, civic commentary, court filings, legal briefs, oral argument, or ordinary public debate about judicial philosophy, constitutional interpretation, court reform, public policy, or litigation strategy.

It does not create a private right of action.

It does not authorize courts to alter the merits, jurisdiction, remedy, timing, or enforceability of any judicial ruling because of an alleged violation.

## How was this done?

The Act is drafted as a structural communications-integrity statute. It focuses on official agency authority, agency adoption, record-tethered disagreement, correction, preservation, oversight, and future-facing compliance architecture.

The proposal is also informed by the Constitutional Physiology / Structural Flow principle that institutional repair requires changed future carry: correction must preserve memory, alter practice, and make later official action answerable to what was corrected.

## Where next?

This model can support legislative discussion, institutional integrity work, judicial-independence advocacy, executive-branch communications policy, inspector-general oversight design, and broader public-law education.
